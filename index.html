LifelinePi

LifelinePi was the startup I cofounded in the spring of 2020. After interviewing healthcare professionals and patients we identified three major barriers to telehealth for older adults: smart device availability, comfort with technology, and internet connectivity. I developed a business model and prototype that solved and removed the first two barriers by providing healthcare systems with a device that could be set up in older adults living rooms and did not depend on a complicated user interface. Our first iteration consisted of a Raspberry Pi that could video call a doctor through a patient’s home television by stitching together existing video calling apps, automated sign-in, and an HDMI connection.
